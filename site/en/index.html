An early methodology for human–AI software development

KGAIDKnowledge-Governed AI-Assisted Development

An open, technology-independent concept for developing software through explicit knowledge, bounded AI assistance, human authority, traceable decisions, and evidence-based completion claims.

Knowledge governs the work. AI assists the work. Humans authorize the work.

Early concept · draft 0.1

This is the beginning, not a finished standard.

KGAID is currently a first structured draft of a methodology. It is being developed while it is used in real software projects, so practical findings can challenge and improve the concept.

  • No certification scheme
  • No claim of industry maturity
  • No fixed toolchain or AI provider
  • Repository documents remain the source of truth

Development status: KGAID is experimental and actively evolving. Some documents in the repository are accepted within the current 0.1 baseline, but the methodology as a whole is still being discovered, tested, corrected, and generalized.

Why KGAID exists

Software can now be produced faster than shared understanding.

AI can accelerate analysis, design, implementation, and review. It can also accelerate hidden assumptions, inconsistent decisions, and confident claims that exceed the available evidence. KGAID starts from the idea that project knowledge must govern this speed.

01

Code is not the whole truth

Product intent, business rules, architectural decisions, contracts, risks, and evidence carry meaning that implementation alone cannot preserve.

02

AI needs bounded authority

AI may analyze, propose, implement, test, and review. It must not silently define product meaning, accept risk, or approve its own proposals.

03

Completion needs evidence

A build, test, review, or deployment proves only what it actually examined. “Done” must be connected to a scoped claim and proportionate evidence.

Human–AI collaboration

Execution capability does not grant decision authority.

The current KGAID reference workflow separates authority, knowledge work, and execution. One person or one AI tool may support several roles, but their meanings remain distinct.

Human authority

Sets purpose and accepts consequences

Humans own product direction, authoritative business meaning, significant decisions, risk acceptance, baselines, releases, and final acceptance.

Knowledge & review AI

Builds context and challenges gaps

AI helps structure knowledge, compare sources, identify contradictions, prepare bounded tasks, review outputs, and explain evidence and limitations.

Execution AI

Realizes an authorized task

AI changes code, tests, documentation, or configuration within accepted knowledge, explicit scope, allowed actions, and mandatory escalation boundaries.

A practical workflow may use a human, ChatGPT as a knowledge and review partner, and Codex as an execution partner. KGAID itself is independent of those products and providers.

Dependency of meaning

From product stimulus to operational learning.

The sequence below is not a mandatory waterfall. Work may overlap and iterate, but downstream commitments should not silently redefine missing upstream knowledge.

PM-1Orient
PM-2Frame product or change
PM-3Discover business and domain
PM-4Define requirements and quality
PM-5Shape architecture
PM-6Establish contracts
PM-7Realize increment
PM-8Verify and baseline
PM-9Operate and learn

Core principles

Twelve principles, summarized into six practical themes.

The repository contains the complete normative wording. This page presents a compact orientation, not a substitute for the source documents.

Knowledge before coordinated action

Important purpose, meaning, decisions, constraints, contracts, and evidence should be explicit enough to discover, review, and use.

Purpose before solution

Product outcomes and business meaning guide architecture; architecture guides implementation; contracts define observable obligations.

Authority stays visible

A proposal, observation, assumption, decision, and accepted artifact must not appear equally authoritative.

Trace intent to evidence

Material work should remain navigable from purpose and rules through decisions, code, verification, and operational learning.

Claims do not exceed evidence

Every meaningful claim is limited by its subject, version, environment, method, data, boundary, and known exclusions.

Rigor follows risk

Low-risk reversible work can stay lightweight. High-impact, regulated, long-lived, or difficult-to-reverse work needs stronger controls.

“A claim must not exceed its evidence.”

KGAID begins verification with the claim, not with the tool that happens to be available.

  • A successful build does not prove functional correctness.
  • A unit test does not prove integration or production behavior.
  • A deployment does not prove release readiness or product value.
  • AI confidence is neither authority nor evidence.

How the concept is growing

The methodology and its reference projects evolve together.

KGAID is being extracted and generalized from practical work rather than designed only as theory. Current learning sources include KSeF_2 and 3ksef. These projects provide experience and feedback; they are not normative authorities for the methodology.

1 · Observe

Use the concept on real work

Apply the current baseline to product, domain, architecture, implementation, and verification decisions.

2 · Record

Capture friction and missing concepts

Do not silently rewrite the methodology inside a project. Record gaps, contradictions, and useful local experiments.

3 · Evaluate

Separate local need from universal rule

Review whether an observation belongs to one project, one profile, or the methodology core.

4 · Govern

Accept and version changes deliberately

Only explicit human decisions can change normative knowledge and future baselines.

KGAID is

  • A knowledge-centered software-development methodology
  • A model for bounded, accountable human–AI collaboration
  • A way to connect intent, decisions, contracts, code, and evidence
  • Technology-, architecture-, provider-, and team-size independent
  • Tailorable in proportion to risk and uncertainty

KGAID is not

  • A programming framework or prescribed architecture
  • A fixed sequence of ceremonies
  • A prompt library or documentation generator
  • A system for autonomous AI decision-making
  • A quality certificate or guarantee
